About the chapter

A community of computing students preparing for what's next.

We are the official ACM student chapter at the University of North Carolina at Pembroke — a chartered branch of the world's largest scientific and educational computing society.

Our mission

Bridge the gap between the classroom and the industry.

The UNCP ACM Chapter exists to help every CS, IT, and CIS student at UNC Pembroke discover their path in computing — through hands-on workshops, peer mentorship, hackathons, and direct connections to alumni already working in the field.

We believe that the best engineers aren't built by lectures alone — they're built in groups of friends pulling all-nighters, breaking things, fixing them, and shipping something they're proud of.

Our vision

Every UNCP grad should leave with...

  • 1 A portfolio of real projects on GitHub.
  • 2 At least one hackathon, internship, or research experience.
  • 3 A network of friends, mentors, and alumni in the industry.
  • 4 The confidence to ace any technical interview.

What is ACM?

The world's largest computing society.

The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) is the world's largest scientific and educational computing society, with over 100,000 members across 190 countries. Founded in 1947, ACM brings together computing educators, researchers, and professionals to advance computing as a science and a profession.

As a chartered ACM Student Chapter, the UNCP ACM Chapter operates under the umbrella of national ACM. Our members get full access to the ACM Digital Library, free O'Reilly e-books, Skillsoft online courses, and discounted attendance at conferences like SIGCSE, CHI, and SIGGRAPH.

Visit acm.org to learn more about national ACM.

Our story

A short history of the UNCP ACM Chapter.

  1. 2014

    Chapter founded.

    A handful of CS majors at UNCP gathered in a Lumbee Hall classroom to charter the university's first ACM student chapter.

  2. 2017

    First HackUNCP.

    Hosted our first 24-hour hackathon — 40 students, 12 projects, one very tired faculty advisor.

  3. 2021

    Recognized as ACM Outstanding Chapter.

    National ACM named UNCP one of its Outstanding Student Chapters for community service and growth.

  4. 2024

    Crossed 100 active members.

    Membership doubled after the launch of weekly workshops and the chapter Discord server.

  5. 2026

    New chapter website launched.

    You're looking at it. Built by chapter members with a modern, accessible, mobile-first design.

What we stand for

Our values.

Open by default

Code, knowledge, and opportunity should be shared. Every workshop is free and recorded.

Inclusive

Every major, every background. You don't need a CS degree to belong here — you just need curiosity.

Hands-on

We learn by building. Every meeting walks away with code on a screen, not just slides.

Student-led

Officers are elected by members each spring. Every member gets a vote — and a chance to lead.
